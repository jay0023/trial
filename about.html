ABOUT

Thika Road (commonly known as the Thika Superhighway) is an 8-lane controlled-access highway in Kenya,
linking the capital city of Nairobi with the industrial town of Thika.
The Thika Road forms 50 km of the A2 Highway, which links Namanga at the Tanzania-Kenya border to the Kenya-Ethiopia border town of Moyale.
The Kenyan President Mwai Kibaki officially inaugurated the superhighway connecting Nairobi and Thika in November 2012. The superhighway is expected to boost the economy of the East African Zone.
